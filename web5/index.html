My Personal Journey

A personal value I would like to share with the world


My hope is for the next generation of people to be able to read my journal andunderstand that I fought my entire career to become who I am today. I value a world where regardless of color or sexuality everyone has the same chance. My goal is to break down barriers keeping people of color and women out of tech.

The money I was making as a security professional/ Account manager was... shall we say.. RIDICULOUS but I was still miserable as cliche as it sounds. Money cannot buy happiness

I found my calling during covid when I personally was able to help out my community by provided 35 laptops from parts provided by doners, former clients and well wishers to students at Mcclain and Central free of charge. When my wife at the time(who is a teacher) I realized my talents were needed elswhere.

In my career I have been:

  • successful
  • homeless
  • discriminated> against
  • arrested for doing my job
  • Revered by colleagues
  • Lonely
  • Loved
  • Designing a Non-Profit to provide Cyber Security Services to minority ownend businesses

I do what I do now for my community, my people, and my friends that never made it out of the hood


This is a personal journal created by me to document my journey and my personal values.
